  • Pre-Permian Extinction (approximately 299 to 252 million years ago)

    Early Permain Period

    The Permian period was the last period of the Paleozoic era. It began 299 million years ago and ended 252 million years ago. The Permian period was a time of great change. The continents were moving and the climate was changing.

  • Permian Extinction (approximately 252 million years ago)

    Late Permian Period

    The mass extinction at the end of the Permian period was the largest in Earth's history. It wiped out 90% of all species on Earth. The cause of the mass extinction is not known for certain, but it is thought to have been caused by a combination of factors, including climate change, volcanic activity, and an asteroid impact.

  • Post-Permian Extinction (approximately 252 to 66 million years ago)

    Mesozoic Era

    The Permian extinction marked the end of the Paleozoic era and the beginning of the Mesozoic era. The Mesozoic era is divided into three periods: the Triassic, Jurassic, and Cretaceous periods. After the mass extinction at the end of the Permian period, the surviving species began to evolve and diversify. Also known as the age of the dinosaurs, it was a time of great change and evolution.
