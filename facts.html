The Permian Extinction Facts

Learn more about the Permian extinction with these fascinating facts and figures. From the causes of the extinction to the impact it had on the Earth's ecosystems, these facts will help you understand the significance of this catastrophic event.

The Permian Extinction is the most severe mass extinction event in Earth's history.

With the lack of concrete evidence surrounding the cause the extinction, scientists are yet to reach a certain explanation. Among those possibilities include severe volcanic activity and a string of environmental changes brought on by the formation of Pangea. Some scientists also believe that an impact could have caused the extinction.

The extinction occurred approximately 252 million years ago.

Life on Earth was dramatically reshaped by the extinction. Over 90% of marine species and 70% of terrestrial species were wiped out. The surviving species had to adapt to new environmental conditions, leading to the evolution of new ecosystems.

The Permian Extinction had a profound impact on the Earth's ecosystems. The extinction event led to the collapse of many food chains and the loss of biodiversity. It also triggered a series of environmental changes, including global warming and ocean acidification, which further contributed to the mass extinction.

The extinction lasted for approximately 12,000 to 108,000 years, where the Earth's ecosystems were severely disrupted and many species were erased.
